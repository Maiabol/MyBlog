All about marmots

A marmot, (genus Marmota), is any of 14 species of giant ground squirrels found mainly in North America and Eurasia. These rodents are large and heavy, weighing 3 to 7 kg, depending upon the species.

Marmots are well suited for life in cold environments and have small fur-covered ears, short, stocky legs, and strong claws for digging. The length of their bulky body is 30 to 60 cm, and the short, bushy tail is 10 to 25 cm long. Their long, thick fur is slightly coarse and is generally yellowish brown (usually frosted with buff white), brown, reddish brown, black, or a mixture of gray and white.

Types of marmots

Marmots come in lots of different species such as:

  • Alaska marmot
  • Alpine marmot
  • Black-capped marmot
  • Bobak marmot
  • Forest-steppe marmot
  • Gray marmot
  • Groundhog
  • Himalayan marmot
  • Long-tailed marmot
  • Menzbier's marmot
  • Tarbagan marmot
  • Hoary Marmot
  • Olympic marmot
  • Vancouver Island marmot
  • Yellow-bellied marmot

There are also four extinct species of marmots recognized from the fossil record:

  • Marmota arizonae
  • Marmota minor
  • Marmota robusta
  • Marmota vetus
